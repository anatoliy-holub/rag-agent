Sherlock Holmes is a fictional detective created by British author Arthur Conan Doyle. 
He is known for his logical reasoning, keen observation, and forensic science skills to solve complex cases.

Holmes resides at 221B Baker Street in London, where he works closely with his friend and biographer, Dr. John Watson.

Holmes uses deductive reasoning to analyze clues that others might overlook. 
He often examines crime scenes, studies physical evidence, and reconstructs events to identify criminals.

Dr. Watson is a former army doctor who assists Holmes and documents their investigations. 
Watson admires Holmes’ intelligence but often struggles to follow his rapid thought processes.

One of Holmes’ most famous adversaries is Professor James Moriarty, a criminal mastermind who challenges Holmes intellectually.

Holmes believes that the human brain should store only useful information. 
He avoids memorizing facts that do not help him in detective work.

The detective frequently disguises himself to gather information without being recognized. 
He is also skilled in chemistry, boxing, and violin playing.

Sherlock Holmes first appeared in the novel "A Study in Scarlet" published in 1887. 
The character became widely popular through short stories published in The Strand Magazine.

Holmes approaches investigations with scientific precision. 
He states that once the impossible has been eliminated, whatever remains, no matter how improbable, must be the truth.

Sherlock Holmes remains one of the most iconic detectives in literary history and has inspired numerous films, television series, and adaptations.
